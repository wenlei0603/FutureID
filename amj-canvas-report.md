## Canvas Check

### 1. The Puzzle

**Present Claim:**
The paper identifies a theoretical puzzle: individuals rarely have a singular, coherent vision of their future self, yet the literature assumes "clear and focused" future selves are optimal. The puzzle is how individuals navigate the multiplicity of future possibilities while maintaining career proactivity.

**Guiding Questions:**
- What broad management question does the project address? → How does complementarity among future self representations influence career self-regulation?
- What are you trying to explain? → Why some individuals with multiple future possibilities engage in proactive career behavior while others do not
- Why is this puzzle important? → Increasing career uncertainty makes singular future self visions less viable; understanding multiplicity is timely

**Assessment:** ✅ Strong. The puzzle is clearly articulated, timely, and grounded in both theoretical缺口 and practical relevance.

### 4. Theoretical Constructs & Relationships

#### What (Constructs)
| Construct | Conceptualization | Operationalization |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Future Self | Network of future-oriented cognitive representations | 10 categories (visual, social, behavioral, relational, evaluative, trait-like, events, surroundings) |
| Future Self Complementarity | Mutually beneficial connections in the network | Valued density of helpful ties (network-based measure) |
| Vitality | "Feeling of aliveness and possessing personal energy" (Nix et al. 1999) | 3-item scale (Porath et al. 2012) |
| Career Adaptability | Self-regulatory strengths: concern, control, curiosity, confidence | 24-item scale (Savickas & Porfeli 2012) |
| Proactive Career Behavior | Networking, skill development, career consultation | 9-item scale (Strauss et al. 2012) |

#### How (Relationships)
- Complementarity → Vitality → Proactive Career Behavior (mediated)
- Complementarity → Career Adaptability (direct, H1; mediated via vitality, H2)

#### Why (Mechanisms)
- **Energy spreading:** Activation spreads along network ties (Anderson 1983); complementary ties allow energy amplification
- **Simultaneous access:** Complementarity enables co-activation of multiple future self representations (cf. Rothbard & Ramarajan 2009)
- **Resource generation:** Contrary to "spread too thin" concern (Holland et al. 1980b), complementarity generates rather than drains resources

**Assessment:** ✅ Well-developed. The network-based theoretical lens is novel and coherent. The mechanism (energy spreading via associative networks) is plausible and grounded in cognitive science.

### 6. Research Design & Analysis

**Design Elements:**
- Study 1: Qualitative generation + quantitative validation
- Study 2: Hierarchical linear regression, PROCESS macro, 10,000 bootstrap estimates
- Study 3: Random assignment, manipulation checks, t-tests
- Study 4: Cluster-randomized field experiment (by workshop), cluster-robust SEs

**Analysis Appropriateness:**
- Mediation: PROCESS macro appropriate for Study 2
- Clustering: Addressed in Study 4 (clustered by workshop, not individual)
- Pretreatment controls: Used in Study 4 to address unexpected baseline differences

**Assessment:** ✅ Rigorous. The preregistration (AsPredicted.org), OSF data sharing, and cluster-robust inference are best-practice elements.

### 7. Empirical Findings

| Hypothesis | Study 2 | Study 3 | Study 4 | Overall |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| H1: FSC → Career Adaptability | Supported | — | Not supported (no total effect) | Mixed |
| H2: FSC → Career Adaptability via Vitality | Not supported | — | Supported | Mixed |
| H3: FSC → Proactive Career Behavior | Supported | — | Supported (scale + behavioral) | Supported |
| H4: FSC → Proactive Career Behavior via Vitality | Supported | — | Supported | Supported |

**Key Patterns:**
- Vitality mediation: Consistent for proactive career behavior (H4 supported across studies)
- Career adaptability: Inconsistent — direct effect in Study 2, indirect via vitality in Study 4, but no total effect in Study 4
- Effect sizes: Comparable to established predictors (career identity, future self salience)

**Assessment:** ✅ Robust for the primary theorized mechanism (vitality → proactive career behavior). Career adaptability findings are legitimately nuanced and honestly reported.

### 8. Contributions

**Three Major Contributions (stated):**

1. **Future self complementarity vs. singular future self assumption**
   - Challenges careers literature assumption that "clear and focused" is always optimal
   - Introduces complementarity as distinct from salience
   - Allows for multiplicity without needing to discard alternatives

2. **Vitality as mechanism**
   - Introduces vitality to careers literature
   - Reframes multiplicity as resource-generating rather than resource-draining
   - Connects positive organizational scholarship to career research

3. **Quantitative identity work measurement**
   - Network-based methodology for capturing complementarity
   - Enables quantitative tests of identity processes
   - Addresses Caza et al. (2018b) call for quantitative identity work measures

**Assessment:** ✅ All three contributions are substantive and non-trivial. The methodological contribution (#3) is particularly valuable for advancing the field.

## Stronger Elements

- **Theoretical grounding:** Network approach to future selves is novel and well-grounded in associative network theory (Anderson 1983, Carlston 1992)
- **Methodological rigor:** Multi-study design with preregistration, OSF sharing, cluster-robust inference
- **Puzzle clarity:** The challenge to "clear and singular" assumption is compelling and timely
- **Mediation evidence:** Causal chain design provides strong evidence for vitality mechanism
- **Contribution statement:** All three contributions are distinct and theoretically significant

## Gaps

- **Career adaptability inconsistency:** The mixed findings for H1/H2 across studies are not fully explained. Authors suggest "career adaptability may be less susceptible to short-term changes," but this is post-hoc
- **Cross-lagged autoregressive model:** Only tested in supplementary study with n=290; results show FSC→PCB significant but FSC→CA not significant — suggesting possible trait-like stability of career adaptability
- **Cultural/generalizability boundary:** All samples from Western, educated populations (UK, Belgium, Netherlands); applicability to non-Western career contexts unknown
- **Alternative mechanisms not ruled out:** Cognitive flexibility proposed but not tested; rule-based vs. associative processing mentioned but not empirically distinguished
- **Intervention sustainability:** Study 4 shows effect 1-4 weeks post-workshop; longer-term effects unknown

## Questions To Resolve

1. Why does career adaptability not show consistent direct effects of FSC, while proactive career behavior does? Is career adaptability more trait-like and thus less susceptible to short-term manipulation?
2. How does the network approach handle dynamic changes in future self complementarity over time — does complementarity build through repeated identity work?
3. What individual differences predict who develops complementary future selves vs. fragmented ones?
4. Could a "complementarity intervention" be delivered at scale, and would effects persist beyond 4 weeks?
5. Is the mechanism truly energy-spreading (as theorized) or could cognitive broadening/bounded rationality also explain findings?

## Next Writing Moves

1. **Discussion:** Strengthen the explanation for why career adaptability shows different patterns — consider distinguishing "state-like" vs. "trait-like" malleability
2. **Future Research:** Add explicit research questions on:
   - Temporal dynamics of complementarity development
   - Cross-cultural validation of the network approach
   - Individual differences (cognitive complexity, need for closure)
3. **Practical Implications:** Expand on how organizations could implement complementarity-focused career development interventions at scale
4. **Boundary Conditions:** Add explicit discussion of career transition contexts as the most appropriate setting for future self complementarity effects
